29-31 July 2016

Manchester City Player Performance Hack

Join us to hack some of the most exclusive data in football and help us understand player performance in brand new ways. What will you discover?

Join us at the City Football Academy to experience our training and performance set up as we host you for an unforgettable weekend during which you’ll have access to exclusive data and insights.

In another world first, Manchester City (MCFC) is opening up incredibly detailed match data and inviting you to create new insight, innovation, visualisations and products.

For this unique Hackathon held at the City Football Academy, located at the Etihad Campus, data partners Opta and ChyronHego will release previously unavailable data and APIs featuring in-match events, player performance and detailed biometric tracking.

We’ve tonnes of exclusive surprises in store, including access to behind the scenes performance analysis systems, training facilities and biometric technologies. You really don’t want to miss this.

We’re looking for the most talented, insightful, innovative and creative programmers, designers, visualisers & innovators. To work alongside them, we’d also welcome sports scientists and data analysts.

TWO TRACKS

The Premier Hack

For experienced data scientists, developers, designers and product *strikers* to get straight on the pitch - you’ll get full access to all the data and APIs to get stuck in over 48 hours.

The Academy

Digital innovation experts and event supplier Play will facilitate a prototyping workshop for over 16s and students new to hacks. Come along and pick up new skills in an incredibly dynamic environment.

The Data

We’ve been collating a huge amount of data, some open, some closed and Opta and ChyronHego are collaborating with us to provide in game data gathered from 10 of the games played during the last Premier League season.

The material we’ve sourced is cutting edge and unfiltered, meaning you’ll have the best possible information and tools available to you.

There will be an early release of the data to successful registrants a couple of weeks before the hack and mentors and SME's made available pre event.

Outline of what the data is

  • For each game, movement, position & speed data for all players, officials & the ball, in total 4.5 million data points per game
  • Game events, event positions, patterns of play, pass & pass outcomes data
  • Shot, goalkeeper & defensive data
  • Line up, substitutions & formation data
  • Referee actions, stoppages & attendance data

FAQ

What is a Hackathon?

Hackathons have long been staple events in the Technology Industry since the late 1990s. A Hackathon is an intense event that brings together people who love tech, including software developers, graphic designers and user interface specialists along with industry process experts and professionals to identify issues and create software solutions, usually within a weekend. For this hack we are opening up new commercially valuable football data, and will be looking for new ideas, insights and opportunities related to football.

Who should register?

Highly skilled people who love solving problems and finding opportunities, they might also love data, football and technology and not necessarily in that order!

The key qualities we are looking for are relevant skills, curiosity, enthusiasm and drive.

The Academy - We are also looking for young tech talent who want to learn more about designing and prototyping solutions, so we will be on hand to support a small group of over 16s who want to make something incredible.

Selection criteria

We are aiming to build small teams of 5 to solve some hack challenges. The selection will be made on suitability, skills and approach to the registration.

Beyond this, due to the limited number of places, if over subscribed we will prioritise those who can demonstrate something interesting that they’ve discovered, made or want to make.

What do I bring?

Bring your own laptop. Sample hack data will be available a couple of weeks before the event and mentors and SME's made available to warm up with.

This will be a weekend to remember for all.

For those who wish to 'sleep in the office' do please bring a sleeping bag and toiletries.

Great performance food and drink will be provided throughout the weekend.

What is the goal of the Hackathon?

We want to create a creative, fun and exciting environment where MCFC can connect with technology designers and developers and create new solutions.

We know it takes longer than a weekend to build great solutions, but this is ample time to form teams of good people and begin creating great solutions which might be commercially interesting for you and for the club.

How do I form or join a team and why?

The team from Play will help form teams of people with complementary interests and skills.

If you would like to start a team or are looking for a team to join, be sure to attend the Friday evening mixer or make an announcement at the opening ceremony of the event. We will also be hosting online discussion and collaboration forums in advance of the event, where you’ll be able to preview some of the data available, and meet some of your potential team members.

If you want to register as a ‘pre-formed’ team, let us know, there are a very limited number of places for this available.

What are the prizes?

There's a cash prize fund available to the Judges to award for the best team, idea or approach.

Participants may be offered work experience / mentoring with CFG at CFG's discretion.

Who owns my work and IP?

We are proposing a very open model:

  • You own the IP you generate at the Hack.
  • The data provided for the hack will be for the duration of the hack only and you will be expected to delete it after the hack.
  • All work created during the hack must be handed over to CFG before the participants leave the event with CFG stipulating that they will not use this for external commercial purposes without consent from the participants who have created the new IP.
  • If you wish to pursue any commercial development in respect of this intellectual property then CFG has a right of first refusal to collaborate with you.
